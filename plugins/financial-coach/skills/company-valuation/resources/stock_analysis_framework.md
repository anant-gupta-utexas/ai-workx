# Unified Stock Analysis Framework

This comprehensive framework integrates the "Set It & Forget It" methodology (high-quality moats), "Tiered Valuation," "Capital Efficiency" strategies, and multi-dimensional assessment approaches (Size, Sector, Cycle) into a single actionable system.

---

## **Phase 1: Qualitative Moat & Market Sentiment Assessment**

Before looking at financials, classify the company by its **competitive durability** and how the **market currently perceives** it.

### **A. The Moat Assessment (The 4% Rule)**

Only 4% of stocks drive all market returns. Identify if the company has:

| Moat Type | Description | Examples |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Switching Costs** | Does the service become "non-discretionary"? | CrowdStrike, ServiceNow |
| **Network Effects** | Does value increase with more users? | Visa, Uber |
| **Cost Advantage** | Can they underprice at scale? | Amazon, Costco |
| **Intangible Assets** | Brand power or specialized IP | Nvidia, Disney, IonQ |

### **B. The Sentiment Tiering (Market Psychology)**

Classify the stock's current "vibe" to determine your entry strategy:

| Tier | Description | Examples | Entry Strategy |
|------|-------------|----------|----------------|
| **1. Trash Bin** | High-quality companies currently "thrown away" due to bad sentiment or temporary disruption fears | Adobe, Salesforce | Best for aggressive buying |
| **2. Dislocated** | High-quality stocks trading noticeably below intrinsic value despite strong fundamentals | Mastercard, Meta, Amazon, Netflix | Strong buy opportunity |
| **3. Fair Value** | Companies priced appropriately for their growth | Google, ASML, Duolingo | Hold or gradual accumulation |
| **4. Premium/Blueberry** | Elite compounders that rarely go on sale. You pay for certainty | Microsoft, Visa | Buy only on dips |
| **5. Cult Status** | Valuations with no historical precedence; require extreme, multi-year growth to justify | Tesla, Palantir | Speculative only |

## **Phase 2: Company Context Assessment (Size, Sector, Cycle)**

To build a robust investment thesis, you must look at a company through three distinct lenses: **Scale (Size)**, **Vertical (Sector)**, and **Timing (Macro Cycle)**.

### **A. Assessment by Company Size (Market Cap)**

Size dictates the risk profile and the primary objective of your investment (wealth preservation vs. aggressive growth).

| Feature | **Small/Mid-Cap (Under $10B)** | **Large/Mega-Cap (Over $100B)** |
|---------|--------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Primary Metric** | Revenue CAGR & Net New ARR | Operating Margins & Free Cash Flow |
| **What to Look For** | Evidence of "Agile Disruption." Is the company taking market share from legacy players? | "Efficiency Gains." Can they grow earnings faster than revenue through cost-cutting or scale? |
| **Risk Focus** | **Cash Burn / Runway.** Small companies can go to zero if they run out of cash before reaching profitability. | **Law of Large Numbers.** Can they still find meaningful growth when they already dominate the market? |
| **Valuation Tool** | Price-to-Sales (P/S) or Forward PEG Ratio | EV/EBITDA or Forward P/E |

### **B. Assessment by Sector (Vertical Dynamics)**

Standard metrics like P/E ratios are often useless when compared across different sectors. Use these "North Star" metrics instead:

#### **1. Technology & SaaS** (e.g., UiPath, Duolingo, Amazon)

| Metric | Description | Target |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| **LTV/CAC Ratio** | Lifetime Value of a customer vs. Cost to Acquire them | >3x is gold standard |
| **Net Retention Rate (NRR)** | Does the company make more money from existing customers each year? | >100% (ideally >120%) |
| **AI Integration** | Is AI a **feature** (easily copied) or a **moat** (integrated into core workflow)? | Moat preferred |

#### **2. Healthcare & Biotech** (e.g., Hims & Hers, Merck, AbbVie)

| Metric | Description | Consideration |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **Patent Cliff & Pipeline** | When do top-selling drugs lose exclusivity? | Must have "Skyrizi-level" replacements |
| **Regulatory Resilience** | Can the company survive price-capping or lawsuits? | Diversification matters |
| **DTC Scale** | For disruptors, track Subscriber Growth and AOV (Average Order Value) | Growth trajectory |

#### **3. Consumer & Financials** (e.g., On Holding, S&P Global, Lyft)

| Metric | Description | Target |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| **Gross Margin Stability** | High-growth brands must maintain margins to prove they aren't "buying" growth with discounts | >60% for premium brands |
| **Net Interest Margin (NIM)** | For banks, the spread earned between lending and borrowing | Sector-dependent |
| **Take Rate** | For platforms, what percentage of every transaction do they keep? | Higher is better |

### **C. Assessment by Economic Cycle (Macro Lens)**

The macro environment tells you whether the wind is at the company's back or in its face.

| Cycle Phase | Strategy | Focus Sectors | Key Metric | Additional Considerations |
|-------------|----------|---------------|------------|---------------------------|
| **Early Expansion (Recovery)** | Aggressive Growth | Cyclical Stocks (Industrials, Materials, Tech) | Forward Guidance—look for double-digit revenue jumps | Consumer confidence returning |
| **Late Expansion (Peak)** | Quality & Value | Market Leaders with pricing power (Amazon, Nvidia) | PEG Ratio—ensure you aren't overpaying for slowing growth | Watch for margin compression |
| **Contraction/Recession (Trough)** | Defensive Income | Consumer Staples & Utilities (Merck, S&P Global) | Dividend Coverage Ratio & Debt-to-Equity | Prioritize "Fortress Balance Sheets" |

**Cycle-Specific Considerations:**
- **High-rate environments:** Prioritize "Fortress Balance Sheets" (e.g., Microsoft's $80B cash) over high-leverage firms
- **Sector Sensitivity:** Financials (JPM, SoFi) are vulnerable to interest rate caps; Tech (Nvidia) is sensitive to capex cycles

## **Phase 3: Capital Efficiency & Financial Integrity (The "Clean" Numbers)**

Go beyond surface-level Net Income. Focus on the actual cash generated and how efficiently it is used.

### **A. The "Clean" FCF Check**

| Check | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **SBC Offset (Stock-Based Compensation)** | Subtract SBC from Free Cash Flow. If FCF is $10B but SBC is $4B, your real FCF is much lower. | Meta has high SBC; Mastercard has minimal SBC relative to FCF |
| **Operating Leverage** | Look for revenue growing faster than expenses. This "flywheel" allows FCF per share to grow at 30% even if revenue only grows 15%. | Scaling software businesses |

### **B. The Efficiency "Smoking Guns"**

| Metric | Target / Benchmark | Why It Matters |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| **ROIC (Return on Invested Capital)** | **>10-15%** | Proves management is creating value, not just spending cash |
| **Rule of 40** | (Revenue Growth % + FCF Margin %) **>40** | Balance between growth and profitability |
| **Asset-Light Moats** | Prioritize "Toll Bridge" businesses | Grow with economy without massive capital expenditures (Visa, Mastercard) |

## **Phase 4: Contextual Risk Assessment**

Adjust your expectations based on the environment and the "story" the market is telling.

| Risk Type | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Sector Sensitivity** | Understand sector-specific vulnerabilities | Financials vulnerable to rate caps; Tech sensitive to capex cycles |
| **Disruption Narratives** | Distinguish between temporary "cat incidents" (regulatory noise) and actual fundamental erosion | Waymo/Uber noise vs. Adobe's revenue slowing from 15% to 10% |
| **Economic Cycle Position** | Match investment strategy to current cycle phase | High-rate environments favor fortress balance sheets |
| **Market Aggregator Risk** | Does simplifying a complex industry create regulatory or competitive risk? | Zillow, Hims |

---

## **Phase 5: Valuation Models & Return Reality Checks**

Don't rely on just one model. Use the one that fits the company's cash flow profile.

### **A. Valuation Model Selection**

| Model | Best For | Goal | Key Consideration |
|-------|----------|------|-------------------|
| **Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)** | Growth and Mature tech (Amazon, ServiceNow) | Calculate the present value of all future cash | Use a "Margin of Safety" (MOS) of at least 10-20% |
| **Dividend Discount Model (DDM)** | Income stocks and Banks (JPM, Exxon) | Value the stock based on sustainability and growth of payouts | Dividend coverage ratio matters |
| **Reverse DCF** | Checking market sentiment | Work backward from current price to see what growth rate the market is "baking in" | If market expects 5% but you see 12%, stock is undervalued (e.g., Uber) |
| **The "Blue Tunnel" (Fair Value Range)** | Visualizing historical trends | Compare current price to historical P/E and yield averages | Upper end = overvalued; Bottom end = undervalued |

### **B. Key Valuation Metrics**

| Metric | Target / Benchmark | Interpretation |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| **PEG Ratio** | **< 2.0** (Ideally < 1.0) | Is growth priced reasonably? |
| **FCF Yield** | **3.0% - 5.0%** | 3% = Premium valuation; 5% = Deep Value ("Trash Bin") |
| **Reverse DCF Growth Expectation** | Compare to your estimate | If market expects 5% but you see 12%, it's a "buy" |
| **25-Year Outlook** | **"The Buffett Rule"** | Is this a company you can leave untouched for decades? |

### **C. Size-Appropriate Valuation Tools**

| Company Size | Primary Valuation Tool |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Small/Mid-Cap (Under $10B) | Price-to-Sales (P/S) or Forward PEG Ratio |
| Large/Mega-Cap (Over $100B) | EV/EBITDA or Forward P/E |

## **Phase 6: The Composite Assessment Checklist**

### **A. Strategic Questions (Variant Perception & Asymmetry)**

| Question | What You're Looking For |
|----------|------------------------|
| **1. Is it a "Market Aggregator"?** | Does it simplify a complex, disjointed industry? (e.g., Zillow for real estate, Hims for healthcare) |
| **2. Is there "Variant Perception"?** | Do you see something the market is missing? (e.g., market sees UiPath as legacy bot maker, but you see an AI Orchestrator) |
| **3. Is the valuation "Asymmetric"?** | If you are right, could it 10x? If you are wrong, is there a floor? (e.g., Lyft's 1.2x sales multiple) |
| **4. Is the cash flow "Hard to Manipulate"?** | Ignore "Adjusted EBITDA" and look at **Free Cash Flow** to ensure growth is real |

### **B. Final Reality Check Rules**

| Rule | Application |
|------|-------------|
| **The Cult Status Trap** | If a stock requires **50% consecutive annual growth** just to provide a standard 4% return, it is a speculative play, not a core investment |
| **The 4% Rule** | Only 4% of stocks drive all market returns—is this company likely to be in that 4%? |
| **The Buffett Rule** | Would you hold this for 25 years without touching it? |
